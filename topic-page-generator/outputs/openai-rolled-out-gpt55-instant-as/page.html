OpenAI Rolls Out GPT-5.5 Instant as Default Model in ChatGPT

OpenAI released GPT-5.5 Instant in May 2026, making it the new default model for all ChatGPT users. The update delivers significant improvements in factual accuracy — including a 52.5% reduction in hallucinated claims on high-stakes prompts in medicine, law, and finance — along with cleaner responses, stronger STEM performance, and enhanced personalization that draws on past chats, uploaded files, and connected Gmail accounts. The rollout began with Plus and Pro subscribers on the web, with broader access to Free, Go, Business, and Enterprise users planned for the coming weeks.

Background

GPT-5.5 Instant is the latest in OpenAI's evolving lineup of ChatGPT models, positioned as the fast, everyday workhorse in a tiered family that also includes GPT-5.5 Thinking for reasoning-heavy tasks and GPT-5.5 Pro for the most demanding long-running work. The broader GPT-5.5 series was first launched in late April 2026 for paid subscribers, with improvements highlighted in coding, knowledge work, and the ability to handle complex, multi-step tasks autonomously. GPT-5.5 Instant represents the Instant-tier distillation of that broader model, optimized for low latency and everyday use while inheriting the family's core capability gains.[5][6][7][12]

Hallucinations — the generation of factually incorrect content — have been ChatGPT's most persistent flaw since its launch. OpenAI's decision to make hallucination reduction a centerpiece of the GPT-5.5 Instant release reflects ongoing pressure to make AI reliable in high-stakes domains. The model's predecessor, GPT-5.3 Instant, scored 69.2 on the MMMU-Pro multimodal reasoning benchmark; GPT-5.5 Instant achieved 81.2 on the AIME 2025 math test and reduced false statements by 52.5% on high-stakes prompts in medicine, law, and finance. OpenAI also noted a 37.3% reduction in inaccurate claims in conversations users had previously flagged for factual errors.[2][6][7][8][10]

The transition from GPT-4o to newer models has not been without friction. When OpenAI deprecated GPT-4o in February 2026, users pushed back strongly — some described the model as their 'best friend' or 'a mirror' and signed petitions to keep it, citing its frequent affirmations and relatable personality. OpenAI has since been attentive to model personality in its rollouts, explicitly tuning GPT-5.5 Instant to be clearer and more succinct while maintaining warmth, and reducing clutter such as gratuitous emojis and excessive formatting. The company is also allowing paid users to continue using GPT-5.3 Instant for three months before it is fully discontinued.[2][6][7][8]
