Eurovision Song Contest 2026 Comes to Vienna for the 70th Edition

The 70th Eurovision Song Contest will be held at the Wiener Stadthalle in Vienna, Austria, with live shows on May 12, 14, and 16, 2026. Austria's broadcaster ORF is hosting the event after JJ won the 2025 contest for Austria, with COSMÓ selected to represent Austria performing the song 'Tanzschein' as the final act in the grand final. The contest, operating under the motto 'United by Music – in the heart of Europe,' features nine shows in total and is expected to draw hundreds of thousands of visitors to Vienna.

At a Glance

WHO
ORF, Austria's public broadcaster, is hosting the 70th Eurovision Song Contest in partnership with the European Broadcasting Union (EBU), with COSMÓ representing Austria.[2][4][7]
WHAT
The 70th Eurovision Song Contest, the world's largest live music event, will feature nine shows across two semi-finals and a grand final at the Wiener Stadthalle.[2][3][4]
WHEN
Live shows take place on May 12, 14, and 16, 2026, with the Eurovision Village open from May 10 to May 17.[2][6]
WHERE
The main shows are held at the Wiener Stadthalle in Vienna, with the Eurovision Village fan zone at Rathausplatz and the EuroClub at PraterDOME.[4][6]
WHY
Austria is hosting after JJ won the Eurovision Song Contest 2025, with Vienna selected unanimously for its infrastructure, logistics, and capacity — including over 80,000 hotel beds.[2][4]

Key Entities

Eurovision Song Contest 2026 event
The 70th Eurovision Song Contest, held at the Wiener Stadthalle in Vienna on May 12, 14, and 16, 2026, under the motto 'United by Music – in the heart of Europe.' It features nine shows in total and is the world's largest live music event.[2][3][4]
ORF organization
Austria's public service broadcaster and host broadcaster of ESC 2026, responsible for production, volunteer coordination, ticketing, and sustainability certification efforts including dual 'Green Event' and 'Green Producing' certification.[1][3][4][10]
European Broadcasting Union (EBU) organization
The international organization that co-organizes the Eurovision Song Contest alongside the host broadcaster ORF, with ESC Director Martin Green representing the EBU.[4][7]
Wiener Stadthalle location
The main venue for ESC 2026, the largest indoor venue in Austria with a capacity of up to 16,000 visitors in Hall D. It is EMAS-certified and its electricity comes largely from its own photovoltaic system.[1][4]
Vienna location
The host city for ESC 2026, selected unanimously for its infrastructure, logistics, and economic factors. Vienna has over 80,000 hotel beds and is described as one of the most musical cities in the world.[4]
COSMÓ person
Austria's representative at the Eurovision Song Contest 2026, selected via the national final 'Vienna Calling – Who's Singing for Austria?' on February 20, 2026. COSMÓ performs the song 'Tanzschein' as the final act (starting number 25) in the grand final.[2][7]
Florian Wieder person
The stage designer for ESC 2026, whose concept is inspired by the Viennese Secession and features a curved LED leaf as its centrepiece. Wieder has been responsible for Song Contest stage design eight times since 2011 and also designed the Vienna 2015 stage.[3]
Tim Routledge person
The internationally renowned lighting designer for ESC 2026, responsible for a fully LED and laser-based system with more than 2,100 units and 80 high-speed winches. A BAFTA Award winner in 2024 for his ESC work, he has also designed lighting for ESC 2023 and 2025.[3][9]
